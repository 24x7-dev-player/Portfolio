Introduction:

  • Diffusion Models generate new data: Like images, similar to what they're trained on.
  • They work in two stages: Corrupting data and denoising.
  • Corrupting data: Adding noise to existing data.
  • Denoising: Learning to remove the added noise.
  • To create new data: Start with random noise.
  • Apply the learned denoising steps: To progressively clean the noise into new data.

How its Work?

  • Dual-phase mechanism: Forward diffusion and reverse diffusion.
  • Data preprocessing: Clean, normalize, augment, and standardize data.
  • Forward diffusion: Introduce noise by sampling from a Gaussian distribution.
  • Incremental modifications: Add structured noise to capture complex patterns.
  • Reverse diffusion: Recognize and denoise specific noise patterns.
  • Complex reconstruction: Converts noise into meaningful data.
  • The model predicts and removes noise: At each step to generate detailed outputs.

Reverse diffusion misconception:

  • Misconception: Neural network's role in diffusion models is misunderstood.
  • Diffusion model is a parameterized Markov Chain.
  • Neural network predicts entire noise removal at each timestep.
  • At t=600, model predicts noise removal to reach t=0, not t=599.
  • Step-by-step reverse diffusion clarifies the process.

Diffusion model techniques:

  • Key mechanisms driving performance: In diffusion models, score-based generative modeling, denoising diffusion models, and using SDEs to describe noise addition.
  • Mathematical tools: SDEs involve both deterministic and stochastic components.
  • Noise addition process: SDEs detail gradual noise introduction in the data.
  • Blueprint for noise: SDEs specify the behavior and impact of noise on data.
  • Flexibility and adaptability: SDEs can be tailored for different data types and generative tasks.
  • Fundamental framework: SDEs are used in various fields to model stochastic processes.

Score-based generative models:

  • SGMs teach models to reverse noise addition, creating clear images from noisy inputs.
  • They excel at generating realistic outputs.
  • Applications: image generation, speech synthesis, and natural language processing.

Diffusion probabilistic models:

  • DDPMs probabilistically remove noise from data, learning to recover the original data during training.
  • They make educated guesses about the original data distribution and accurately reconstruct it.
  • Applications: Image denoising, speech enhancement, and signal processing.

Popular Diffusion tools:

  • DALL-E 2: Highly detailed image generation from text by OpenAI.
  • DALL-E 3: Latest version integrated into ChatGPT.
  • Sora: OpenAI's text-to-video model for realistic 1080p videos.
  • Stable Diffusion: Known for stable diffusion model architecture by Stability AI.
  • Midjourney: API-based diffusion model for image generation from text prompts.
  • Imagen: Google's text-to-image model for photorealistic outputs.

Architecture:

  • Architecture: Modified U-Net.
  • ResNet Block: Key component in the architecture.
  • Downsample Block: Used for reducing dimensionality.
  • Self-Attention Block: Enhances feature representation.
  • Summary:
    • 1, Forward diffusion adds noise using a schedule (e.g., linear).
    • 2, Noise can be added in one step with a specific equation.
    • 3, Reverse diffusion removes noise in multiple steps.
    • 4, Diffusion model predicts the entire noise.
    • 5, Inference can use a different schedule and number of steps.
    • 6, Uses sinusoidal encoding and text embedder for timestep and prompt.
    • 7, Some ResNet blocks are replaced with Self-Attention blocks.
