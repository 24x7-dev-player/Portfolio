Exploratory Data :Analysis

Why do EDA?

  • Modeling: Build predictive or descriptive models.
  • Analysis: Interpret data for trends.
  • Validation: Confirm data assumptions.
  • Missing Values: Manage and impute missing data.
  • Feature Engineering: Create new data features.
  • Outlier Detection: Identify unusual data points.

Column Types:

  • Numerical - Age,Fare,PassengerId
  • Categorical - Survived, Pclass, Sex, SibSp, Parch,Embarked
  • Mixed - Name, Ticket, Cabin

Univeriate Analysis

  • Univariate analysis focuses on analyzing each feature in the dataset independently.
  • Distribution analysis: The distribution of each feature is examined to identify its shape, central tendency, and dispersion.
  • Identifying potential issues: Univariate analysis helps in identifying potential problems with the data such as outliers, skewness, and missing values.
  • The shape of a data distribution refers to its pattern on a graph, such as Normal, Skewed, Bimodal, or Uniform.
  • Dispersion measures, including Range, Variance, Standard Deviation, and Interquartile Range (IQR), describe the spread of data.
  • These characteristics help identify outliers, skewness, and inform statistical tests and models.

Steps of doing Univariate Analysis on Numerical columns:

  • Descriptive Statistics: Compute summary statistics (mean, median, mode, etc.) to understand the data's distribution and identify outliers or skewness.
  • Visualizations: Use histograms, box plots, and density plots to visualize the data distribution and identify outliers or skewness visually.
  • Identifying Outliers: Examine outliers to determine if they're errors or valid data points, deciding whether to include or exclude them.
  • Skewness: Check for skewness and consider transforming the data or using robust statistical methods if skewness is present.
  • Conclusion: Summarize findings and decide on further analysis steps based on the exploration.

Steps of doing Univariate Analysis on Categorical columns:

  • Descriptive Statistics: Compute the frequency distribution of categories to understand their distribution and relative frequencies.
  • Visualizations: Use count plots and pie charts to visualize the category distribution and identify patterns or anomalies.
  • Missing Values: Check for missing values and decide whether to impute or exclude them based on the research question and dataset.
  • Conclusion: Summarize the findings and decide on further analysis steps based on the exploration.

Steps of doing Bivariate Analysis:

  • Select 2 columns: Choose two columns from your dataset for analysis.
  • Understand type of relationship:
    • Numerical - Numerical:
      • Plot graphs like scatterplot (including regression plots), 2D histplot, 2D KDEplots.
      • Check correlation coefficient to assess linear relationship.
    • Numerical - Categorical:
      • Create visualizations comparing the distribution of numerical data across different categories of the categorical data.
      • Plot graphs like barplot, boxplot, kdeplot, violinplot, and scatterplots.
    • Categorical - Categorical:
      • Create cross-tabulations or contingency tables showing the distribution of values in one categorical column, grouped by values in the other categorical column.
      • Use plots like heatmap, stacked barplots, treemaps.
